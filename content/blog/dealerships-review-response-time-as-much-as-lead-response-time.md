# Your Dealership's Review Response Time Matters as Much as Your Lead Response Time

When you respond to a lead in [under five minutes](https://www.visquanta.com/blog/speed-to-lead-why-it-matters-in-car-sales), your chances of converting them skyrocket. Reviews work the same way. Ignoring them sends a message to customers - and it’s not a good one.

**78% of buyers choose the first business that responds.** Yet, 87% of businesses don’t even reply to negative reviews. That’s a massive missed opportunity. Quick, thoughtful responses don’t just build trust. They directly impact revenue, search rankings, and customer loyalty.

Here’s the deal: 60% of reviewers expect a reply within two days. A one-star improvement in your rating can boost revenue by 5%–9%. And 63% of unhappy customers will update their negative reviews if you resolve their issues.

If you’re not prioritizing review responses, you’re leaving money - and customers - on the table. It’s time to treat reviews like leads. Answer fast, address concerns, and watch your reputation and revenue grow.

## The 24-Hour Response Rule for All Reviews

You already know the importance of responding to leads within five minutes. Now, take that same urgency and apply it to customer reviews. Quick responses don’t just convert leads - they build trust and protect your bottom line.

Here’s why it matters: 60% of consumers expect a reply to their review within two days[\[3\]](https://moz.com/learn/seo/responding-to-customer-reviews). The dealerships dominating local search and winning customer loyalty treat reviews with the same level of urgency as they do leads.

Make it a rule: respond to every review within 24 hours. For negative reviews, acknowledge the issue, apologize, and take the conversation offline. Mixed reviews? Address the concerns while reinforcing the positives. And for positive reviews, show gratitude, personalize your response, and encourage the customer to return. Remember, 90% of consumers pay attention to how businesses respond to reviews[\[3\]](https://moz.com/learn/seo/responding-to-customer-reviews)[\[11\]](https://moz.com/local-business-review-survey-report/power-of-owner-responses). Speed counts, but thoughtful, tailored responses carry even more weight - so skip the canned, automated replies.

The benefits are clear. When you respond and resolve issues, 62% of customers are willing to give you another shot[\[2\]](https://moz.com/local-business-review-survey-report)[\[11\]](https://moz.com/local-business-review-survey-report/power-of-owner-responses). Even better, 63% of them will update their negative review once their complaint is resolved[\[2\]](https://moz.com/local-business-review-survey-report)[\[11\]](https://moz.com/local-business-review-survey-report/power-of-owner-responses). That’s how you turn a 1-star review into a 5-star one - by acting fast and following through.

### Responding to Negative Reviews: Acknowledge, Apologize, Take It Offline

Negative reviews are a chance to show future customers how you handle problems. A quick, thoughtful response can turn a bad situation into a win. Use this three-step approach: acknowledge the issue, offer a sincere apology, and move the conversation offline for resolution[\[3\]](https://moz.com/learn/seo/responding-to-customer-reviews). Never argue or get defensive.

For example:  
"We’re sorry to hear about your experience with the service wait time. That’s not the standard we aim for. Please call me directly at \[phone number\] so we can make things right."

> As Joy Hawkins from Sterling Sky puts it, "Lots of them deal with negative reviews by personally contacting the person and trying to resolve their issue. In my experience, it's actually the most successful tactic for getting rid of a negative review"[\[11\]](https://moz.com/local-business-review-survey-report/power-of-owner-responses).

When handled privately, many customers will either update or remove their negative review altogether.

### Responding to Positive Reviews: Show Gratitude, Personalize, and Invite Them Back

Positive reviews are like free advertising - but only if you respond. While only 40% of customers expect a reply to their positive feedback[\[3\]](https://moz.com/learn/seo/responding-to-customer-reviews)[\[11\]](https://moz.com/local-business-review-survey-report/power-of-owner-responses), responding thoughtfully can leave a lasting impression. Avoid generic replies. Instead, personalize your response by mentioning specifics, like the vehicle they purchased or the service they received. Bonus: integrating relevant keywords can help boost your local SEO.

For example:  
"Thank you for choosing us for your F-150’s oil change and tire rotation! We’re thrilled our service team could assist you. We look forward to helping you with your next maintenance appointment."

Research shows that 51% of consumers actively look for businesses that take the time to craft personalized responses[\[11\]](https://moz.com/local-business-review-survey-report/power-of-owner-responses).

### Responding to Mixed Reviews: Address Concerns and Highlight the Positives

Three-star reviews aren’t failures - they’re opportunities. They often point out areas for improvement while highlighting what you’re doing well. Respond within 24 hours by addressing the complaint and reinforcing the positive feedback. This shows you’re listening and committed to getting better[\[3\]](https://moz.com/learn/seo/responding-to-customer-reviews).

For example:  
"We’re glad you enjoyed working with our friendly sales team - that’s exactly the experience we strive for. We’re sorry the financing process took longer than expected. We’ve shared your feedback with our F&I team to improve the process. Please don’t hesitate to reach out at \[contact info\] if there’s anything else we can do."

This type of response acknowledges the issue, takes accountability, and protects your reputation - all without sounding defensive.
