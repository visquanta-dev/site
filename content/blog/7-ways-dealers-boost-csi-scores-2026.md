## What Makes CSI Scores Rise or Fall in 2026?

48% of vehicle owners leave a service visit frustrated - that's the number from the 2023 Cox Automotive Service Study, as reported by fixed-ops consultancy Chris Collins Inc. (chriscollinsinc.com). Every dealer principal already feels it: the service lane is where loyalty is won or lost. Fixed operations now drives more than half of total dealership gross profit. One bad service experience doesn't just tank a survey score - it puts the store's most reliable revenue stream at risk heading into 2026 ([according to Chriscollinsinc](https://chriscollinsinc.com/sdr/fixed-ops-made-easy-expert-dealership-consulting/)).

[Fixed ops](https://www.visquanta.com/service-drive) accounted for 50.5% of total dealership gross profit as of 2019 (Mercer Capital). By 2026, that share has only climbed as new-vehicle margins compress. Every point of CSI erosion in the service lane threatens the single largest profit center most rooftops have. The seven tactics below each target a documented failure point - slow follow-up, disengaged advisors, ignored digital leads - that drags satisfaction down and pushes owners toward independents.

## 1. Switch First-Contact Service Updates to Text - What Does the Data Show?

### Why Text Is Replacing the Phone in Service Lanes

J.D. Power's 2023 U.S. Customer Service Index Study flagged the shift: dealerships are moving to text over [phone calls](https://www.visquanta.com/blog/dealership-call-management-strategies). Chris Collins Inc. highlighted the same trend in its fixed-ops strategy recommendations (chriscollinsinc.com). The logic is simple. A text lets the customer read and respond on their own timeline. A phone call demands immediate attention and usually hits voicemail. For service departments processing dozens of status updates per hour, texting frees advisors to stay on the drive instead of chasing callbacks - a capacity gain that compounds across every RO.

### Night Owls Submit Forms Too

CDK Global's modern-retailing research confirms what lead boards show every morning: customers browse online at night and submit forms during work hours (cdkglobal.com). That behavioral gap - research after dinner, act during lunch - means the first-response window is narrow and unpredictable. Stores defaulting to phone-only follow-up call when the customer is unavailable and never reconnect. A text or email reply queued for the moment the form lands keeps the conversation alive. It signals the store operates on the customer's schedule, not the other way around ([as reported by Cdkglobal](https://www.cdkglobal.com/insights/modern-retailing-what-you-need-know)).

## 2. Reactivate Lapsed Service Customers - How Did One Store Hit 80% Retention?

### The 127-Owner Outreach That Changed Everything

From the reactivation campaigns we've run across franchise and independent stores, nothing matches a disciplined BDC working a lapsed-owner list. McGuire's team identified and contacted 127 vehicle owners who hadn't visited the service lane in 7 to 12 months (CDK Global). The outreach was direct, personal, and systematic. Each owner got a follow-up designed to bring them back for overdue maintenance ([per Cdkglobal](https://www.cdkglobal.com/insights/grow-your-service-business-through-your-bdc-0)).

The [results](https://www.visquanta.com/case-studies) were dramatic. McGuire's 7-to-12-month first-service return rate hit 80% (CDK Global). That figure represents the share of buyers who came back to the selling dealer for their first maintenance appointment. In a business where first-service return predicts long-term loyalty, 80% is exceptional.

That last data point matters: the surrounding market wasn't close. The northern New Jersey average sat at 65.8%. The New York zone average came in at 64.4% (CDK Global). McGuire's BDC-driven reactivation [campaign](https://www.visquanta.com/custom-campaigns) beat the district by roughly 15 points. Outbound follow-up is not optional - it is the mechanism that separates retention leaders from the pack.

## 3. Close More Maintenance Leads Through Your BDC - Why Does a 9-Point Gap Matter?

### A 9-Point Closing Advantage

When a [BDC](https://www.visquanta.com/blog/ai-bdc-guide-for-car-dealerships) handles maintenance leads with urgency, the numbers move fast. McGuire's closed dealer maintenance leads at 39% in November 2017 (CDK Global). The district average that month was 30%. A 9-point gap in closing rate means dozens of additional ROs per month - each one a chance to deliver a positive experience and lift CSI.

### Higher Close Rates Drive Higher Revenue per Visit

The closing advantage didn't just increase volume. It raised the value of each visit. McGuire's average monthly RO billing hit $198 versus a district average of $191 (CDK Global). The $7 difference per RO sounds modest. Multiply it across hundreds of monthly orders and the revenue impact compounds fast. Faster lead handling brings in customers ready to authorize work - not customers who already solved the problem elsewhere.

### Volume Tells the Story of Opportunity

McGuire's BDC processes roughly 50 to 60 inbound service calls and another 15 for sales each month (CDK Global). That is not a massive call center. It is a focused team working a manageable pipeline with discipline. Talk to any GM running a BDC the way we do and they'll tell you the same thing: you don't need 20 people to move the needle. You need a process that treats every inbound [contact](https://www.visquanta.com/book-demo) as a revenue opportunity and a satisfaction touchpoint.

## 4. Treat Every Online Lead as a Live Deal - How Does This Shift CSI?

### Stop Ignoring Online Leads

Too many stores still push the in-store appointment above all else. They ignore online leads and offers. They force customers to follow the path the store wants them to take (CDK Global). In 2026, this approach is a direct [CSI](https://www.visquanta.com/blog/dealerships-ai-improve-csi-scores) killer. Customers who feel dismissed online arrive already frustrated - or they never arrive at all.

### Every Lead Is a Live Deal

The fix starts with a mindset shift. Every lead has to be worked as a deal - because in reality, it may be one (CDK Global). An internet lead is not a lesser form of customer contact. It is a buyer signaling intent. Stores that treat digital submissions as warm handshakes rather than c[old data](https://www.visquanta.com/blog/ai-revives-dormant-crm-data) points convert more and frustrate fewer.

### The Trust Multiplier That Closes Deals

CDK Global's modern-retailing guide - originally published on the eLead CRM platform - frames the stakes bluntly: becoming a trusted consultant makes it 100 times more likely a customer will buy from you (cdkglobal.com / elead-crm.com). The claim is directional, not derived from a controlled study. But the underlying principle holds across every metric we track. Across the rooftops we work with, the pattern repeats: when an advisor answers pricing questions without deflection and lets the buyer set the pace, objections shrink and post-purchase survey scores climb. CSI doesn't begin when the vehicle rolls onto the lift. It begins the first time a lead gets a straight answer.

## 5. Invest in Employee Engagement - Can a 21% Global Rate Survive in Service?

### The Engagement Problem No Dealer Can Afford

Fixed-ops consultant Chris Collins Inc. cites Gallup data: global employee engagement has dropped to 21% (chriscollinsinc.com, referencing Gallup's State of the Global Workplace report). 52% of employees are watching for or actively seeking a new job. Those figures describe every industry, but the damage is amplified in a service lane. Each advisor interaction directly shapes a CSI survey response. A disengaged advisor rushes the walk-around, skips the follow-up text, and generates exactly the friction that sends an owner to the independent shop. Fixing the customer experience starts with stabilizing the workforce delivering it.

### What 95% Approval Looks Like

Contrast that baseline with organizations that invest in culture. One company earning Great Place to Work certification for a third consecutive year reported 95% of employees affirming it is a great place to work. The typical U.S. company averages 57%. That 38-point gap is not abstract. It shows up in how employees greet customers, handle complaints, and go beyond the minimum ([research from Prnewswire](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/creative-services-inc-csi-background-screening-services-achieves-great-place-to-work-certification-for-the-3rd-year-in-a-row-301932225.html)).

### Respect Over Office Politics

Creative Services, Inc. (CSI) - a background-screening firm with Great Place to Work certification three years running - reported that 100% of its employees said colleagues prioritize treating each other with equality and respect over engaging in office politics (PR Newswire). Different industry. Same principle. Sit across from enough service managers and one thing stops being a surprise: when advisors and techs trust each other, information flows faster. A tech flags an unexpected finding. The advisor communicates it clearly. The customer gets a transparent update instead of a surprise line item. Internal respect is invisible to the vehicle owner, but it determines whether every interaction feels competent or chaotic.

## 6. Capture the 62% Who Want to Return - Why Do High-Tech Owners Defect?

### 62% Want to Come Back - So Why Don't They?

Chris Collins Inc. highlights a Cox Automotive finding: 62% of consumers with high-tech vehicles would prefer to have their cars repaired by the original seller (chriscollinsinc.com, citing the 2018 Cox Automotive Fixed Ops Study). That majority isn't leaving because an independent offered a lower price. They leave because the selling dealer never followed up after the sale, made online scheduling difficult, or failed to communicate during the repair. Each lost return visit is a CSI survey that never had a chance to register a positive score - and a gross-profit dollar that walked out the door with the owner.

### The 5% Retention Rule

Chris Collins Inc. references a widely cited Bain & Company theory: increasing customer retention by just 5% can boost profits by as much as 95% (chriscollinsinc.com, citing bain.com). The exact multiplier varies by segment. The directional math doesn't. Retaining an existing service customer costs a fraction of acquiring a new one and yields higher per-visit revenue - returning owners authorize more work. Having sold cars on the floor myself, the pattern we see across our rooftops is clear: the highest-leverage CSI investment in 2026 is not a cosmetic waiting-room upgrade. It's a systematic retention engine that identifies owners drifting toward defection and re-engages them with proactive, personalized outreach before the relationship lapses.

## 7. Redesign the Sales Process Around Customer Experience - What Does Modern Retailing Require?

### From Sale-at-Any-Cost to Trusted Consultant

Having sold cars on the floor myself, the pattern we see across our rooftops is clear: the stores winning on CSI redesigned the sales process itself. Moving from a sale-at-any-cost mentality to a trusted consultant approach is how you win today's customers (CDK Global). This is not a surface-level script change. It requires rethinking how leads are handled, how pricing is presented, and how follow-up is structured. Every other tactic on this list works better when the underlying process respects the buyer's autonomy.

### Get Involved Before the Customer Arrives

Modern retailing demands involvement from the beginning. CDK Global emphasizes rethinking the traditional road to a sale by changing internal processes to implement the customer experience you want. That means engaging with digital leads before a customer ever walks through the door. Build the relationship in the inbox or text thread so the in-store visit feels like a continuation - not a cold start. Dealers who redesign around this principle see higher satisfaction at every stage of ownership.

### Accept the Floor - Then Raise It

No process redesign will achieve perfection. As one veteran dealer principal put it: not everyone can be 100% satisfied. That honest admission is not defeatism. It is the starting point for realistic improvement. The goal is raising the floor so the baseline experience - the one delivered on a busy Saturday with two advisors out sick - still meets the standard. Build systems around that constraint. Dealers who do consistently outperform those chasing an impossible ideal ([Autonews reports](https://www.autonews.com/article/19940124/ANA/401240716/commissioner-of-communications-dodge-seeks-to-put-more-information-in-the-hands-of-nada-s-members/)).


## The Bottom Line

CSI improvement in 2026 is not about survey gaming - it is about closing documented process gaps in follow-up speed, lapsed-owner outreach, and digital lead handling that directly correlate with retention and per-RO revenue.

**What this means for dealerships in 2026:**

- Fixed ops contributes over 50% of gross profit - every CSI point lost accelerates defection to independents.
- A focused BDC working just 50-60 inbound calls monthly can lift maintenance close rates 9 points above district averages.
- Reactivation outreach to 7-to-12-month lapsed owners can push first-service return above 80%, well ahead of typical 64-66% zone averages.
- Text-first communication aligns with after-hours browsing behavior and eliminates the voicemail dead zone.
- By late 2026, stores without systematic digital follow-up will face compounding retention losses as EV service intervals lengthen.

The dealers who operationalize these follow-up loops now will own the retention gap before competitors recognize it exists.
