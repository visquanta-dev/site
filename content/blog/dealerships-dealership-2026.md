## The Stat That Should Worry Every Manual-Process Store

74% of dealership leaders plan to invest in voice agents this year , [according to Digitaldealer](https://digitaldealer.com/news/2026-could-be-the-first-ai-operations-year-as-dealerships-focus-on-tech-overhaul/168666/). That number comes from a pool of nearly 1,200 decision-makers - not a vendor webinar poll. The use cases driving the spend all hit the same nerve: automating lead follow-up, routing inbound calls without a human switchboard, and filling service bays on autopilot. Every one of those attacks the exact spot where labor cost and response speed collide. Voice agents aren't a shiny-object bet. They're the single largest consensus line item across the industry right now.

The voice-agent surge fits a much larger pattern. A 2025 study found 90% of dealerships are either running automation in their operations or have concrete plans to start , [as reported by Nada](https://www.nada.org/nada/nada-headlines/7-ways-technology-helps-auto-dealerships-succeed). The remaining ten percent are not just behind the curve - they are watching it disappear in their rearview mirror.

The survey behind those figures - the Automotive Market Sentiment and Dealer Operations Report - polled close to 1,200 dealership decision-makers across geographies, brand mixes, and store volumes . That's closer to an industry census than a convenience sample. When three-quarters of a respondent pool that size converges on the same technology priority, the signal is structural. Not anecdotal.

## Where Dealers Are Spending Beyond Voice

### Merchandising and Inspection Automation

[Voice agents](https://www.visquanta.com/blog/how-ai-voice-agents-improve-service-retention-and-loyalty-for-dealerships) lead the investment rankings, but they are far from the only line item. Every day a car sits un-photographed on the lot is a day it is invisible to online shoppers.

Beyond the front-end visual experience, dealers are pouring money into the analytical backbone of their operations . Pricing and analytics tools attracted commitments from 62% of surveyed dealers. Sales development platforms for variable and [fixed ops](https://www.visquanta.com/service-drive) followed at 54%. CRM modernization rounded out the top tier at 49%. Taken together - an industry overhauling nearly every customer-facing and back-office system at once.

## Fixed Ops: The Overlooked Revenue Unlock

### Why Fixed Ops Deserves the Same Urgency

Fixed ops is the lifeblood of most rooftops - and it's not close , [per Cbtnews](https://www.cbtnews.com/using-ai-to-drive-more-fixed-ops-revenue/). Service and parts generate recurring revenue that persists even when new-vehicle sales soften. But this profit center is under mounting pressure: rising customer expectations, evolving vehicle technology, and independent service chains eating market share. Automation offers a path to defend and grow that revenue without proportionally scaling headcount.

One of the clearest applications is predictive maintenance scheduling . Machine-learning algorithms now process telematics data from [connect](https://www.visquanta.com/integrations)ed vehicles to forecast optimal service windows before a customer even notices a problem. Instead of waiting for a check-engine light or a mileage reminder, the dealership reaches out proactively with a targeted appointment offer timed to the vehicle's actual condition.

Predictive scheduling gets interesting when it triggers the right offer at the right time. Ongoing data monitoring lets stores assemble targeted replacement packages around components with the highest wear signals - brakes nearing pad limits, batteries losing cold-cranking capacity, tires approaching tread thresholds . Because the recommendation is anchored to the vehicle's actual condition - not a generic mileage interval - customers read the outreach as useful maintenance guidance instead of an upsell. Approval rates on recommended work go up.

The personalization extends beyond scheduled maintenance. Automated systems analyze detailed customer and vehicle data to build profiles generating tailored service recommendations for each owner . A customer driving mostly highway miles gets different guidance than one navigating stop-and-go city traffic. The result: higher approval rates on recommended work and stronger per-RO revenue - all without adding a single advisor to the payroll.

- Predictive scheduling based on telematics data
- Personalized wear-and-tear replacement packages
- Tailored service recommendations by driving profile
- Higher repair order revenue without additional [headcount](https://www.visquanta.com/blog/ai-isnt-replacing-car-dealership-jobs-its-transforming-them)

## The Digital Buyer Expects More Than a Website

### Mostly-Digital Buyers Are Happier Buyers

Tech investments only pay off if they match what buyers actually want. Cox Automotive's 2025 Car Buyer Journey Study found that buyers who handled at least half their transaction steps online - financing, trade-in valuation, paperwork - reported higher overall satisfaction than those who completed fewer steps digitally . That reframes the entire automation conversation. Digitizing the purchase path isn't just an efficiency play for the dealer. It directly elevates the experience customers say they prefer - turning process speed into a measurable satisfaction advantage.

Platforms built around that preference are already posting dramatic lifts. Autotrader's conversational browsing mode - which surfaces inventory through intent-driven discovery rather than static filter menus - produces 3x the lead volume of conventional search, and its [integrate](https://www.visquanta.com/integrations)d shopping assistant converts shoppers at 6x the standard rate . Here's the disconnect between those two numbers worth noting: the browsing mode captures attention, but the assistant closes it. Layered automation touchpoints compound each other rather than compete.

Consumer willingness to transact has migrated well beyond OEM sites and third-party listing portals. 45% of Americans now say they'd buy their next vehicle through a social media channel . That turns Instagram, TikTok, and Facebook into de facto research-and-intent engines - places where shoppers compare trims, watch walkarounds, and form purchase opinions before a dealership domain ever loads. For stores still treating social as a branding afterthought, the risk is concrete: they're invisible to nearly half the market during the highest-intent phase of the buying cycle.

## Five Steps to Catch Up Before Q3

### Step 1: Frame Automation as a Resource, Not a Replacement

The fastest way to sabotage an automation rollout is to let your team believe it threatens their jobs . Successful implementations start by positioning these tools as resources that eliminate mundane, repetitive tasks so employees can focus on higher-value work. When advisors and [BDC](https://www.visquanta.com/blog/ai-bdc-guide-for-car-dealerships) reps understand the technology handles the tedious call routing and data entry they already dislike, adoption accelerates and resistance drops.

### Step 2: Benchmark Against Projected Margin Compression

Before selecting any tool, quantify the cost of inaction . With projected 25% reductions in profit per vehicle retailed this year, every process inefficiency that survived the last cycle becomes a direct threat to your bottom line. Map your current cost-per-lead, cost-per-appointment, and BDC expense ratios against the benchmarks early adopters are already hitting - let the gap define your investment priority list.

1. Calculate your current cost-per-lead and cost-per-appointment
2. Compare BDC operating costs to the 33% reduction benchmark
3. Model the impact of a 25% PVR decline on your current margins
4. Identify the two or three process areas where automation closes the largest gap
5. Set a 90-day adoption timeline with measurable checkpoints

### Step 3: Prioritize [24/7](https://www.visquanta.com/blog/why-your-service-department-needs-24-7-call-answering-not-just-voicemail) Coverage Tools First

Chatbots handling after-hours inquiries, digital browsing platforms serving personalized vehicle recommendations, credit applications and paperwork at any hour - these always-on capabilities ensure no lead goes cold overnight and no service appointment request waits until Monday morning . The stores that capture demand at 10 PM will outperform the ones that return the call at 9 AM. Start with one workflow. Measure everything. Expand what works.
